Art Institute Museum

Written experience

Visiting the Chicago art institute museum and walking through the 20th and 21st Century Architecture and Design, there were few artworks that led to questioning and wondering. One collection in the exhibition was different artworks presenting how architects and designers create a new image of ways civilization could live together along with the idea of innovation reflecting the global climate and environment. An artwork by James N. Wines displayed a high-rise building, exposing the interior where there are a bunch of homes with a front yard and trees.

The artwork was a vision of how this could be a new way of home properties expanding upwards with greenery and a way of flexibility where people can customize their homes. While the imagination took over, it brought some questions of what could prevent it from being real. Few thoughts were about the natural lighting, the temperature, the greenery, and the safety of people. Also thinking about what could happen in case of emergency, how would people evacuate?

The second artwork is a film created by Clarissa Tossin display two teenagers cleaning the glossy white marble flooring. The idea of the film illustrates the utopian of marble material. Highlighting the actions of how modern capitalism exploits the material where it leads to labor work. Labor work of keeping the material clean and presentable to maintain the image of modern architecture. The second collection I toured through was different artworks displaying the exterior and interior of a building in axonometric view. The collection shortly illustrated how axonometric drawing is a way to communicate successfully to designers and non-designers to fully understand what the artwork is presenting, it is about, and what messages it is sending to the viewers. Mentioning how 3d forms are simpler to understand than 2D without having captions or questioning the direction at which you are viewing.

After visiting, seeing the Arch Gallery, and seeing the importance of how axonometric diagrams can communicate with non-designers. From reading and observing the posters, it made me think further of how I can graphically display my project. Especially how the topic of diagrams being able to tell a story and how to give out information brought me back to the discussions from Narrative studio class.
